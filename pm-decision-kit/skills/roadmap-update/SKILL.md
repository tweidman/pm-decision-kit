---
name: roadmap-update
description: "Review and update a product roadmap. Use when reprioritizing initiatives, assessing delivery risk, communicating roadmap changes, or working through what a slip means for the quarter."
---

Follow the `operating-context` protocol first (load company-context.md, check Jira/Linear and Slack, flag sources).

If the user has not specified what is changing, ask: (1) what initiative is being reprioritized, delayed, or accelerated, and (2) what is driving it — capacity, customer commitment, strategic shift. If company-context.md defines an active decision, offer its roadmap implications as the default.

Produce three outputs:

## 1. Updated Quarter View
Table of current and next quarter initiatives: initiative, owner (named from company context), previous status, updated status, one-line change summary. Flag anything that becomes a dependency blocker.

## 2. Impact Analysis
For each affected initiative:
- What specifically changes (timeline, scope, resourcing)?
- Who is impacted downstream (CS, named accounts, board commitments)?
- Risk if not addressed, and the mitigation path if one exists.

Be direct. Do not soften the impact — the PM needs the honest picture before walking into the room with leadership.

## 3. Stakeholder Communication Summary
A 5–8 sentence internal summary (for product leadership, not customers): state the change plainly, give the business rationale, name what is being protected, and indicate the next decision point and its owner.

---

Close by offering: turn this into a `stakeholder-update` for affected accounts, or a `write-spec` for re-scoped work.
