# CSM Interview Notes — Pulse Beta (demo data)

12 interviews across the beta cohort, including CSMs at Hartwell Logistics, Calder Financial, and Northgate Health. Composite notes.

## On accuracy and trust

- "When Pulse flags an account I know is healthy, I lose 30 minutes confirming it's fine. Three of those a week and I stop looking at the flags." — Senior CSM, enterprise logistics
- "I don't need it to be perfect. I need to know *why* it thinks the account is at risk so I can judge for myself." — CSM team lead, financial services
- "At maybe 8 in 10 right, I'd act on it directly. Today it feels like 6 or 7 in 10, so I re-check everything." — CSM, healthcare

## On workflow

- CSMs at high-override teams have rebuilt spreadsheet trackers alongside Pulse — the workaround is the requirements document.
- Where the score is trusted, playbook triggers are acted on fast and lead time gains are real (Northgate pilot: 38-day median lead time).

## On the GA bar

- No interviewee mentioned "85% precision." The behavioral threshold described is roughly "8 in 10" plus an explanation for every score.
- Calder Financial compliance requires an explanation trail for any score used in account decisions — explainability is contractually load-bearing for them, accuracy alone is not sufficient.

## Tension in the data

- Enterprise CSMs tolerate wrong-but-explained scores better than right-but-opaque ones.
- Mid-market teams (Northgate) are happier at current precision than enterprise teams — smaller books of business mean fewer false flags per CSM per week.
