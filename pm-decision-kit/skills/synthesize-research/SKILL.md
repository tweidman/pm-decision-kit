---
name: synthesize-research
description: "Synthesize user research, interviews, or survey data into actionable PM insights. Use when you need to find patterns, generate themes, build a research-backed recommendation, or ground a decision in qualitative evidence."
---

Follow the `operating-context` protocol first (load company-context.md, search Notion/Confluence and Slack research channels if connected, flag inferred vs. actual data).

If the user has not specified research, ask: (1) what we're working with — interviews, surveys, session data, support tickets — and (2) what decision or document the synthesis feeds. Default to research files listed in company-context.md Data Sources.

Produce:

## Research Synthesis: [Topic]
**Research type:** […] | **Participants / data points:** [note if inferred vs. actual] | **By:** [PM persona] | **Date:** [today] | **Feeds into:** […]

### Key Themes (3–5)
For each: theme name + one-line framing; 2–3 sentences on the pattern; who holds this view (role/segment, not names if confidential); the PM implication for the decision at hand.

### Supporting Quotes
3–5 verbatim or paraphrased quotes anchoring the themes. If working from inferred data, use representative composite quotes and label them as such.

### Contradictions or Tensions in the Data
What did participants disagree on? What cut against the dominant theme? This section is where PM judgment lives — surface the complexity, do not smooth it over.

### Assumptions This Research Tests
Map findings back to beliefs: which assumptions under the current plan does this research **support**, which does it **undercut**, and which does it **not speak to at all**? The third category is where teams most often overclaim. Hand these to `assumption-map` for a structured pass.

### PM Recommendation
What should the product team do? Be specific: the change that most directly addresses the top theme, what to prioritize now vs. defer, and what the data says about any contested target or threshold. Written as a recommendation deliverable to product leadership in a 10-minute sync.

---

Close by offering: a `write-spec` for the recommended change, a `stakeholder-update` for a specific audience, or an `assumption-map` to formalize what's still unvalidated.

---

### Frameworks Behind This Skill
Theme extraction per continuous discovery interviewing (Teresa Torres, *Continuous Discovery Habits*). Tensions-in-the-data discipline per qualitative research norms. Jobs-to-be-done probing (Anthony Ulwick; Bob Moesta) where interview structure applies.
